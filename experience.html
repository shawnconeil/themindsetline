The Mindset Line

This was a mirror that was turned on a blind spot I had in my life.

This was given to me by a mentor that admittedly changed his life, and this also changed my life.

I can tell a lot about someone—about their life and their business—about where they live their life, above or below the mindset line.

Above the Line

Victors

People who win in life. A lot. Happy.

Below the Line

Victims

Above the Line

Ownership

If you wear it, you ate it. Which means if I am overweight, it's because I ate it. I own the fact that I made that decision.
Below the Line

Blame

Everyone in my family is fat. It's genetics. There is no way that I could ever have six-pack abs. Genetics.
Above the Line

Accountability

I check in with my trainer every single Sunday. Pictures from four angles. Weight. My diet. Hold me accountable. I'm accountable to my results.
Below the Line

Excuses

I travel six days a week. I'm in an airport all the time. I live in a hotel. I don't have time. I can't do that.
Above the Line

Responsibility

I'm responsible to the people in my life that I make commitments to. I have to live my life this way. Because I'm responsible for the outcome.
Below the Line

Denial

It's not me. Someone else's responsibility.

What does this look like?

In practical application. In life and business.

Above
  • Sees possibilities
  • Finds better ways
  • Seeks solutions
  • Proactive
  • Forward-looking
Below
  • Only sees problems
  • Finds fault
  • Reactive
  • Defensive
  • Always sees the negative
My business was terrible. And in fact, I was homeless. And even suicidal. Because this is where I lived.

It's my wife's fault.

Genetics. I was poor.

My father didn't love me.

My kids don't love me.

Somebody told me, you need to read a book.

Extreme Ownership. By Jocko Willink.

Got on a plane. Flew to Phoenix, Arizona. Listened to him give his speech. Brought chills in my life. Raked down my spine.
I chose my wife. I married my wife. I decided to stay when she treated me a certain way. I decided to stay. I didn't find ways to improve it.
I own the fact that I was miserable in my unhappiness. That was me. I chose that.
If it's me, it's my power. If I'm blaming them, I'm giving them the power. I can't do anything about it. I am powerless. Because it's someone else.
But if I own it—I messed up. I did that. I chose that. I stayed. I failed. Boom. I own it. Now I have the power to accept the responsibility to change it.

How do you feel?

Above the Line
  • Empowered
  • Enthusiastic
  • Compassionate
  • Calm. Steady.
  • Relaxed. Confident.
Below the Line
  • Frustrated
  • Dissatisfied
  • Suspicious of everyone
  • Resentful
  • Fearful. Always tense.

This is no way to live a life.

I was laying in a jail cell and I decided—I own this. Because I drink every day. Because I'm not taking the responsibility to deal with my PTSD. I'm in denial. I'm making excuses why I'm such a shithead in life.
Now I know who I am. I know my faults. I'm accountable to them. I'm not perfect. And I'm working to solve those things. Because I'm responsible. And I'm empowered. Because I have the power to do it.
No one else has power over me. No more.
The only solution is always me.
And you.

So I encourage you. No matter where you are right now. I lived as far below that line as you can get. Now—this feels really, really amazing.
